Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a traditional Roman pasta dish made with a few simple ingredients: eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper. Creamy without using cream, this quick dish is rich, savory, and incredibly satisfying — a perfect example of Italian culinary simplicity.

This recipe combines freshly cooked spaghetti with crispy pancetta and a silky sauce made from eggs and Parmesan. Tossed together while still hot, the pasta becomes coated in a creamy, cheesy mixture that clings to each strand without overwhelming it.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti: The base of the dish. Cook until al dente.
  • Eggs: Used to make the creamy, rich sauce.
  • Parmesan cheese: Freshly grated for best flavor.
  • Pancetta: Adds a salty, meaty crunch.
  • Black pepper: For a warm, spicy kick.
  • Salt: For seasoning the pasta water.

Steps

  1. Cook the spaghetti in salted water until al dente.
  2. While the pasta cooks, sauté pancetta until crisp.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, Parmesan, and pepper.
  4. Drain the pasta and return it to the pan (off heat).
  5. Quickly stir in the egg mixture and pancetta, letting the heat of the pasta cook the sauce.
  6. Serve immediately with extra cheese and pepper.
