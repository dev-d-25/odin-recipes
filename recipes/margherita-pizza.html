Margherita Pizza

Description

Margherita Pizza is a classic Italian pizza that highlights the beauty of fresh, high-quality ingredients. With its vibrant colors and clean flavors, it's a delicious homage to the Italian flag — featuring red tomatoes, white mozzarella, and green basil.

This recipe starts with a thin, crisp pizza crust topped with tangy tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella slices, and fragrant basil leaves. Baked at high heat, the cheese melts beautifully and the crust becomes perfectly golden.

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough: Store-bought or homemade.
  • Tomato sauce: Use fresh, simple tomato sauce for authentic flavor.
  • Fresh mozzarella: Sliced into rounds for even melting.
  • Fresh basil: Adds aroma and a pop of color.
  • Olive oil: Drizzled on top before baking.
  • Salt: Just a pinch to enhance flavor.

Steps

You’ll find the full, step-by-step recipe below — but here’s a brief overview of what you can expect when you make Margherita Pizza:

  1. Preheat the oven to its highest temperature (around 475–500°F).
  2. Roll out the pizza dough on a floured surface.
  3. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the base.
  4. Add slices of mozzarella and scatter fresh basil leaves.
  5. Drizzle with olive oil and a sprinkle of salt.
  6. Bake until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling.
